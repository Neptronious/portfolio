04 Tensions in the Journey

What pushes them away — what pulls them in.

Two forces shape the experience in equal measure.

Barriers

What erodes trust at the counter.

B1

Counter friction

Long queues, unclear rules, dismissive staff.

B2

Forced purchases

Pressure to buy something when cashing out feels coercive.

B3

Crime & safety

Receivers hide cash, vary pickup times — never feel safe.

B4

Banking distrust

Hidden charges, scams & stolen funds stories keep them out.

Drivers

The non-negotiables that win loyalty.

D1

Proximity

A nearby outlet beats almost everything else.

D2

Predictability

Consistent rules. Money there when promised.

D3

Safety

Protection from scams, theft & staff negligence.

D4

Respect

Dignity — especially for elderly & Indigenous receivers.

05 Cultural Backdrop

Why this corridor still runs on cash.

Banking is growing in Mexico, but cash remains how this corridor breathes. Four reasons surfaced again and again.

Tradition & sharing

Tangible, familiar, easy to divide across family and community.

Safety & control

"I keep it at home and tell my family where to find it."

Mistrust of banks

Hidden fees, "stolen" funds, suspicions of collusion with crime.

Fear of exposure

Undocumented senders & off-the-books receivers avoid scrutiny.

09 Design Outcome

Remesas — inside the app people already trust.

The pillars shape a new entry to remittance in the Walmart Mexico app, beside the wallet and store credit they already use weekly.

One known surface

Lives next to Cashi & Walmart Pass — no separate app, no separate login.

Plain, predictable language

"Dinero de Estados Unidos a México" — descriptive, never jargon.

Cash-friendly by default

Pick up at 3,000+ Walmart stores customers already shop at.

Transparency upfront

Exchange rate & total receivable shown before the sender commits.
